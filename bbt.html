Basal Body Temperature

Basal Body Temperature (BBT) is your body’s lowest resting temperature, typically measured right after waking and before any activity.

After ovulation, progesterone causes your BBT to rise slightly (typically 0.3–0.5°C). Tracking your BBT over time can help confirm that ovulation has occurred.

How to Track:
• Use a basal thermometer (more sensitive than standard thermometers)
• Take your temperature at the same time each morning before getting out of bed
• Record it daily in the tracker
• Look for a sustained rise in temperature for at least 3 days
